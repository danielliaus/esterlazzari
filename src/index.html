About me

Hi there! I am postdoctoral research fellow at the University of Vienna. Prior to moving to Austria, I completed my PhD in demography at the Australian National University in Canberra. My research focuses on the causes and consequences of late fertility and on the ways in which medically assisted reproduction affects fertility trends. My work has examined the impact of assisted reproduction on family size, with a particular focus on its contribution to the recovery of births at older ages. Other of my research interests include fertility desires and intentions, social age norms for childbearing, and the association between education and fertility. My research has been published in demography and medical journals such as Demographic Research, Human Reproduction, and Population Studies.

PHD THESIS: Childlessness, delayed childbearing and assisted reproduction

April 2022

The focus of my PhD thesis is to understand and analyse the ways in which assisted reproduction, conceptualised as a contextual driver of fertility recuperation, affects fertility recovery in the context of delayed childbearing using Australia as a case-study. Demographic methods and tools, including survival analysis, decomposition techniques, and projections, are used to study the factors associated with delayed childbearing and childlessness, the contribution of assisted reproduction to current and future fertility levels and the barriers to its utilisation. Results from this work show that in the present context of ongoing postponement of the steps leading to family formation, assisted reproduction may well increase its contribution to fertility rates in the future and become a crucial force behind the recuperation of births at older ages.
See thesis.

IMPACT OF POLICIES ON FERTILITY RATES

March 2022

What is the impact of government policies on fertility decisions? The Centre for Population of Australia commissioned this report to gain insights into the measures that should be considered to support parenting to prevent a rapid decline in fertility. The report demonstrates the importance of policies that provide stability and support for raising children, for participation in employment through parental leave and childcare, and that reduce the financial costs for parents. It also points to the importance of shared gender roles supported by public policies which support both parents’ involvement in work and family, through the availability of leave and the provision of childcare. Assisted reproductive technology broaden the range of possible responses to low fertility rates, although its future impact on fertility rates remains uncertain.
